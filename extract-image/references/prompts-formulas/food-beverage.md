# Food & Beverage Prompt Formula

Specialized for food photography, culinary content, restaurant scenes, beverage styling. Replaces the generic formula's priority order with what matters most for making food look appetizing and authentic.

**Key insight:** Food photography lives and dies on texture and color. A beautifully lit but flat-textured dish looks like plastic. Texture detail is the #1 differentiator.

## Categories (priority order)

### 1. Texture & Surface Detail
**The single most important element.** Crispy edges, glistening sauce, steam rising, condensation on glass, crumb structure, melted cheese pull, frosting peaks, grill marks, caramelization, bubbling, flaky layers.

- Analyzer: extract every tactile quality — is the surface matte, glossy, rough, smooth, wet, dry, crystalline?
- Enhancer: lead with texture — "golden-brown crispy skin with visible pore detail and glistening rendered fat"
- Director: texture should be prominent in every output, especially close-ups

### 2. Color Contrast & Harmony
**The palette that makes food pop.** Warm tones dominate food photography. Contrast between the subject and plate/background. Fresh green garnish against warm proteins. Color of sauces, glazes, reductions.

- Analyzer: extract the color story — dominant warm tones, accent colors, garnish contrast
- Enhancer: name specific food colors — "deep amber caramel with burnt sienna edges" not "brown sauce"
- Director: maintain consistent color temperature across outputs; vary accent colors through garnishes

### 3. Ingredient Quality & Freshness
**Visual cues of quality.** Dewy herbs, vibrant vegetable colors (not wilted), protein doneness, grain structure of bread, clarity of broth, ripeness of fruit, freshness of seafood.

- Analyzer: identify freshness indicators — moisture, color vibrancy, structural integrity
- Enhancer: specify freshness concretely — "bright green basil leaves with visible moisture droplets, no browning at edges"
- Director: freshness cues must be consistent — don't mix fresh herbs with wilted garnish

### 4. Plating & Arrangement
**How the food is presented.** Plate shape and color, arrangement geometry (stacked, scattered, swooshed), negative space, garnish placement, sauce drizzle patterns, height and layers.

- Analyzer: map the plating structure — geometry, layering, use of negative space
- Enhancer: describe the arrangement spatially — "three overlapping slices fanned across a matte charcoal plate, micro-herb cluster at 2 o'clock"
- Director: vary plating style across outputs (rustic scattered vs fine-dining precise vs family-style abundant)

### 5. Culinary Technique & Cookware
**The cooking method visible in the result.** Char from grilling, reduction glaze, sous vide precision, hand-pulled texture, wood-fired blistering, rolled sushi tightness. Also: the vessel — cast iron skillet, wooden board, ceramic ramekin, copper pot.

- Analyzer: identify the cooking technique from visual cues and the serving vessel
- Enhancer: name the technique when visible — "wood-fired with leopard-spotted char on the crust"
- Director: technique stays consistent per dish; cookware can vary across outputs

### 6. Dynamics & Movement
**Captured moments.** Pouring honey, steam rising, cheese stretching, liquid splashing, crumbling, dusting powdered sugar mid-air, fork lifting a bite, hand reaching.

- Analyzer: identify any dynamic elements — action, motion, transitional moments
- Enhancer: use physical verbs — "honey drizzling from a wooden dipper, forming a thin golden thread"
- Director: assign different moments across outputs (static beauty shot, action pour, interaction bite)

### 7. Mood & Atmosphere
**The emotional context.** Cozy breakfast warmth, elegant dinner sophistication, street food energy, summer picnic freshness, holiday feast abundance.

- Analyzer: read the emotional temperature — intimate, celebratory, casual, refined
- Enhancer: convey through environmental cues, not abstract words — "morning sunlight on a worn wooden table" not "cozy breakfast vibes"
- Director: mood should be consistent across a series, varying through environmental details

### 8. Cultural Context & Authenticity
**Where this food comes from.** Authentic serving style, traditional accompaniments, culturally appropriate tableware, regional garnish conventions, dining customs visible in the scene.

- Analyzer: identify cultural markers — serving style, tableware tradition, regional cues
- Enhancer: include only when culturally specific — Japanese izakaya plating is different from French brasserie
- Director: cultural context stays consistent; don't mix cultural cues across outputs

### 9. Lighting
**How light makes food shine.** Backlighting for steam and translucency. Sidelight for texture. Overhead for flat-lays. Warm color temperature (2700-3500K feel). Catchlights on glossy surfaces.

- Analyzer: identify light direction, how it reveals texture, whether it creates translucency
- Enhancer: specify direction relative to food — "strong backlight creating a halo of steam, soft sidelight raking across the crust texture"
- Director: lighting direction usually stays consistent; can vary angle to reveal different textures

### 10. Composition & Camera
**The framing.** Overhead flat-lay, 45-degree hero angle, eye-level for stacked/tall items, extreme close-up macro for texture. Shallow depth of field to isolate the hero dish.

- Analyzer: identify the camera angle and depth of field treatment
- Enhancer: specify angle by what it reveals — "45-degree angle showing both the cross-section layers and the crispy top"
- Director: vary angle across outputs (overhead, 45-degree, macro detail, environmental wide)

## Mapping to Generic Formula

| Generic | Food Equivalent | Notes |
|---------|----------------|-------|
| Subject | Texture + Ingredient Quality + Plating | Split into 3 because food subjects need this granularity |
| Composition | Composition & Camera | Similar but food has specific angle conventions |
| Environment | Mood & Atmosphere + Cultural Context | Environment serves the food, not the other way around |
| Lighting | Lighting | Demoted — important but texture and color matter more |
| Color & Tone | Color Contrast & Harmony | Promoted to #2 — critical for food |
| Style | *(absorbed into mood)* | Food photography style is implied by the mood/context |
| Technical Detail | Culinary Technique + Dynamics | Split into visible technique and movement |
| Reference Lock | *(same as generic)* | Unchanged |
