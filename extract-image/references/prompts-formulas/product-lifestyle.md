# Product Lifestyle Prompt Formula

Specialized for product placement in styled scenes, brand-driven ad creatives, product shots, e-commerce lifestyle imagery. The domain where **the product is hero** and everything else serves it.

**Key insight:** Product photography is about making one object the undeniable focal point. The environment, lighting, and props exist to elevate the product — never to compete with it. Subject isolation (visual hierarchy) is the #1 priority.

## Categories (priority order)

### 1. Product Identity
**The hero, precisely described.** Physical form, dimensions, proportions, material surface (matte, glossy, metallic, translucent), color, distinctive features (logo placement, texture, opening mechanism, cap shape), condition (new, used, vintage).

- Analyzer: extract every physical attribute — this becomes the fidelity anchor
- Enhancer: lead with the product — "tall cylindrical frosted glass bottle with rose-gold cap and embossed logo at center"
- Director: product identity stays IDENTICAL across all outputs (this is the constant)

### 2. Product-Scene Interaction
**How the product lives in its setting.** What surface it sits on, what it leans against, whether it's held, how it casts shadows, reflections on the surface beneath it, objects adjacent to it, spatial relationship to props.

- Analyzer: describe the physical contact points — what touches what, what reflects where
- Enhancer: specify the interaction — "resting on a rough-cut marble slab, slight reflection on the polished surface, a sprig of lavender leaning against the base"
- Director: vary the interaction across outputs (tabletop hero, hand-held in use, environmental context)

### 3. Lighting for Product
**Light that serves the product.** How light reveals the product's material (translucency, metallic reflections, matte texture), key light direction, fill ratio, whether the lighting is studio-controlled or environmental, catchlights and specular highlights on the product surface.

- Analyzer: trace how light hits the product specifically — not just the scene
- Enhancer: describe light-product interaction — "soft diffused light from upper-left revealing the brushed aluminum grain, single catchlight on the cap"
- Director: lighting should consistently flatter the product; vary the scene lighting (studio vs golden hour vs overcast)

### 4. Visual Hierarchy & Composition
**The product dominates the frame.** Size in frame (hero prominence), depth of field (product sharp, background soft), rule of placement (center, golden ratio, off-center with leading lines), negative space around the product, eye path from product outward.

- Analyzer: assess how prominently the product reads in the composition
- Enhancer: specify the hierarchy — "product fills the center-left third of frame, shallow depth of field blurring the background scene, ample negative space right for text overlay"
- Director: vary composition across outputs (centered hero, off-center lifestyle, macro detail, environmental wide)

### 5. Scene & Environment
**The world the product lives in.** Setting type (studio, home, outdoor, cafe, bathroom, gym), background treatment (solid, gradient, environmental), props that support the product story (not compete), season/time cues.

- Analyzer: identify the scene's purpose — does it tell a story about the product's use context?
- Enhancer: describe the scene relative to the product — "minimalist bathroom shelf with white subway tile background, eucalyptus bunch and folded towel flanking the product"
- Director: vary the scene across outputs (same product in different contexts: studio, lifestyle, in-use)

### 6. Color & Brand Alignment
**Colors that reinforce the brand.** Product color as the palette anchor, complementary or analogous background tones, brand colors appearing in props or environment, contrast level between product and background.

- Analyzer: check if the color scheme serves or fights the product's visibility
- Enhancer: build the palette from the product outward — "warm terracotta background complementing the product's deep green, dried botanicals echoing the gold cap accent"
- Director: color palette stays brand-consistent; vary the specific props/environment colors

### 7. Material Detail & Texture
**Close-up fidelity.** Label legibility, surface finish at macro level, material imperfections that signal authenticity (not defects), packaging details, tactile quality cues (you can feel the weight, the smoothness, the grain).

- Analyzer: zoom in — label text, print quality, surface micro-texture, seal condition
- Enhancer: add material precision for close-ups — "visible micro-texture of the matte rubber grip, crisp debossed logo, hairline seam at the cap junction"
- Director: material detail increases as composition zooms in (wide = less, macro = max)

### 8. Mood & Aspiration
**The feeling the image sells.** Luxury (dark, dramatic, minimal), fresh/clean (white, bright, airy), natural/organic (earth tones, raw textures), energetic (bright colors, dynamic angles), sophisticated (muted tones, editorial composition).

- Analyzer: read the intended emotional register from existing brand materials
- Enhancer: convey mood through concrete choices — "dark moody backdrop with dramatic single-source lighting" not "luxurious atmosphere"
- Director: mood stays consistent across a campaign; the creative brief sets the emotional register

### 9. Props & Styling
**Supporting cast.** Ingredients/materials visible alongside the product, lifestyle objects (magazine, coffee cup, sunglasses), natural elements (flowers, leaves, stones), fabric swatches, seasonal items. Props must be subordinate to the product.

- Analyzer: inventory props and assess whether they support or compete with the product
- Enhancer: props serve the product story — "scattered raw cocoa beans and a halved vanilla pod on the marble surface beside the chocolate bar"
- Director: vary props across outputs to tell different product stories (ingredients, lifestyle, occasion)

### 10. Style & Post-Production Feel
**The photographic treatment.** Editorial, commercial, e-commerce clean, documentary, social-native. Film grain vs clean digital. Color grading (warm, cool, desaturated). High-key vs low-key.

- Analyzer: identify the photographic genre and post-production treatment
- Enhancer: state concisely — "Clean commercial product photograph, high-key lighting, minimal color grading"
- Director: style stays constant across outputs in a campaign
